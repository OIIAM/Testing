Twice's History

Sixteen Era

J.Y. Park, the founder of JYPE, announced on February 11, 2015, that the lineup of the upcoming seven member girl group would be decided through Sixteen, a competition survival show that would air on Mnet later that year.
The show aired from May 5th to July 7th, and the seven Twice members were Nayeon, Jeongyeon, Sana, Jihyo, Mina, Dahyun, and Chaeyoung.
Park then announced that the group size would be increased to nine from seven by adding Tzuyu, a fan favorite, and Momo, who was added by Park himself because he felt the group needed someone with her performance abilities.

2015

Twice made their official debut on October 20th, 2015, with the release of their EP The story begins, which included the lead single "Like Ooh-Ahh," as well as a live show to promote it.
The group received a lukewarm reception at first, but "Like Ooh-Ahh" would peak at number 10 on the Gaon Digital Chart three months after its initial release.
Within five months of their debut, the music video for the group's single had 50 million views on YouTube.
The group's early success earned them the title of "Best New Female Artist" at the 2015 Mnet Asian Music Awards, which were held on December 2nd.

2016

Twice released their second EP, Page Two, on April 25th, 2016, which included the lead single "Cheer Up," a rearranged version of Park Ji-yoon's 1998 hit "Precious Love," and "I'm Gonna Be A Star," which served as the Sixteen theme song.
The group's debut album was a commercial success, debuting at number two on the Gaon Album Chart and number six on the Billboard World Albums chart.
"Cheer Up," the album's lead single, became the group's first chart-topper in South Korea, debuting at number one on eight real-time music charts shortly after its release.ic charts shortly after its release.

2017

Twicecoaster: Lane 2 was released on February 20th, along with its lead single, "Knock Knock."
The album debuted at number one on the Gaon Album Chart and number four on the Billboard World Albums chart.
On February 24, they officially announced their Japanese debut with the release of a compilation album titled #Twice on June 28. Twice released their fourth EP, Signal, on May 15, with the lead track of the same name, produced by Park Jin-young.
Twice digitally released "Signal (Japanese ver.)" along with a short version of its music video on June 14 as a preview and countdown to the release of their Japanese debut album. They released the full music video for the Japanese version of "TT" a week later.
Twice released Twicetagram, their first full-length Korean album, on October 30th, with the lead single "Likey."

2018

On February 7th, 2018, Twice's second Japanese single, "Candy Pop," was released.
The single sold 303,746 copies in its first week and topped the Japan Hot 100 chart, becoming their second single to be certified platinum by the Japan Recording Industry Association (RIAJ).
What is Love?, their fifth EP, was released on April 9th, 2018, with the single of the same name produced by Park Jin-Young.
The RIAJ certified Twice's third Japanese single, "Wake Me Up," as Double Platinum on May 16, 2018.
Twice released their first Japanese studio album, BDZ, on September 12, 2018. On August 17, 2018, its lead single of the same name was pre-released as a digital single.
Twice's sixth EP, Yes or Yes, was released on November 5th, 2018, with the lead single of the same name.

2019

The group released their second Japanese compilation album #Twice2 on March 6, 2019. With 95,825 units sold, the album debuted at number one on the Oricon Albums Chart's daily ranking.
Fancy You, their seventh EP, was released on April 22, 2019.
The music video for "Fancy," which received over 42.1 million views in a single day, also ranked seventh on YouTube's list of the biggest debuts in the first 24 hours.
Twice released two Japanese digital singles, "Happy Happy" and "Breakthrough," on June 12, 2019. "Happy Happy" was released physically on the same day as the digital singles, followed by "Breakthrough" a week later.
On September 23, 2019, Twice released their eighth EP, Feel Special, along with the music video for the lead single of the same name, produced by Park Jin-young. The song was the group's second number one single on the Billboard World Digital Song Sales chart.

2020

On April 20th, 2020, it was reported that the group was preparing to release a new Korean album in June.
The group's ninth EP, More & More, was released on June 1 with a lead singple of the same name.
On August 9th, Twice held their first online concert titled "Twice:World in a Day" in response to their "Twicelights World Tour Finale" which had been canceled earlier in the year.
Twice then collaborated with the virtual girl group K/DA, with members Nayeon, Sana, Jihyo, and Chaeyoung being featured on the song "I'll Show You" as part of K/DA's first EP All Out released on November 6.

2021 - Present

Twice made an appearance on Time magazine's Time 100 Talks series on January 28 via livestream broadcast, delivering a special performance of their track "Depend On You" from their Eyes Wide Open album.
The group held their second online concert, "Twice in Wonderland," on March 6, which was first announced on January 14.
Taste of Love, the group's tenth Korean EP, was released on June 11. The EP's lead single, "Alcohol-Free," was pre-released on June 9, along with its music video.
On October 1, the group released their first official English-language single, "The Feels."
Scientist, the title track from the group's third Korean studio album, was released on November 12.
Twice's contracts with JYP Entertainment were renewed on July 12. On the same day, Twice announced the release of their eleventh Korean EP, Between 1&2, on August 26.
